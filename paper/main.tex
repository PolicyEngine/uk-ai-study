\title{Who bears the AI shock? Displacement, poverty and the public finances in the UK\thanks{Draft --- not for citation.}}

\begin{abstract}
\noindent How much an AI labour shock costs the United Kingdom depends on who bears it. I build the first open-source, full tax-benefit microsimulation of AI displacement incidence for the UK: occupation-level AI exposure is attached to Family Resources Survey workers, shocks are simulated across 66 scenarios, and counterfactual incomes are evaluated with PolicyEngine UK, treating who loses work --- not just how many --- as a scenario axis. In the central scenario, the Exchequer cost is \pounds 18.4 billion a year and before-housing-costs poverty rises by 1.87 percentage points. Holding the aggregate shock fixed, the fiscal cost varies from \pounds 14.2 billion (uniform incidence) to \pounds 24.5 billion (expertise compression); a family shaped by measured UK firm-level displacement patterns costs \pounds 32.5 billion. Who bears the shock decides whether the UK gets a fiscal problem or a poverty problem; either way the Gini rises, in all 66 cells and all incidence families.
\end{abstract}
