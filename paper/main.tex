\title{Who bears the AI shock? Displacement, poverty and the public finances in the UK\thanks{Draft --- not for citation.}}

\begin{abstract}
\noindent How much an AI labour shock costs the United Kingdom depends on who bears it. This is, to my knowledge, the first study to use a full tax--benefit microsimulation model to treat the incidence of AI displacement---who loses work, not only how many---as a scenario axis. I attach occupation-level AI exposure to Family Resources Survey workers and simulate the resulting employment, wage and capital shocks with PolicyEngine UK, across 66 shock-size combinations and five incidence families that hold the aggregate shock fixed while varying who is displaced. In the central scenario the Exchequer cost is \pounds 18.4 billion a year and before-housing-costs poverty rises by 1.87 percentage points. The same aggregate shock costs \pounds 14.2 billion under uniform incidence and \pounds 24.5 billion under expertise compression---\pounds 32.5 billion under a family shaped by measured UK firm-level displacement patterns---while poverty varies far less. Who bears the shock decides whether the UK faces a fiscal problem or a poverty problem; inequality rises either way, in all 66 cells and every incidence family.
\end{abstract}
